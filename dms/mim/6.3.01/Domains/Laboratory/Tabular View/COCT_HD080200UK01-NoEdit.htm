COCT_RM080200UK01
Specimen

This Specimen CMET is derived from the R_Specimen [Universal] in the HL7 v3 ballot. It is designed to be used for general Laboratory requests and reports without the overhead of the requirements of Automation or Specimen Tracking. It is constrained for use with Human subjects and excludes environmental, non-human and manufactured specimens. The 'Specimen' may play the role of a Specimen or an Isolate.

Used by:
[1..1] classCode (CS {CNE:SPEC} ){ Fixed="SPEC" }

Indicates the role being played by the entity.

Entities in the Specimen CMET may play the role of either a 'Specimen' or an 'Isolate'. The RoleClass identifies the type of role being played by the entity in a particular message.

Valid role types are 'SPEC' or 'ISLT' according to the entity playing the role.

Where 'SPEC' is used, the code shall be taken from the Snomed National Specimen Type subset.

Where 'ISLT' is used, the code shall be taken from the Snomed National_Lab Isolate subset_UK20060401.  

[1..1] id (II)

The ID is the unique identifier for the Specimen/Isolate.

According to the use of the CMET, the ID may be a Placer or a Fulfiller identifier for the Specimen/Isolate. In Request interactions, it shall be the Placer identifier, in Event interactions, it shall be the Fulfiller identifier. In Event interactions, provision is made to carry the Placer Identifier in an associated role (SpecimenAlternatIdentifier) in addition to the Fulfiller identifier where one is available.  The ID is made up of two parts, a root and extension. The Root will be an OID allocated to the system that generates the extension and will be at such a level that the combination of the Root and Extension is universally unique. The extension will be a human readable string generated by the system and may not be unique across the whole system.

E.g. There are many systems that are configured on a departmental basis and the same range of human readable strings are used in multiple departments. In such cases, the Root OID shall be allocated at such a level that the human readable portion is unique within that Root OID.

< id root =" 9.99.999.99.10.2 " extension ="B05.00504885C "/>

Where 9.99.999.99.  identifies the Hospital organization and 9.99.999.99.10.2 identifies the ID scheme within the Laboratory system that generated the extension B05.00504885C

[1..1] code (CE {CNE:SnomedCT} )

NOTE: This datatype is currently restricted by HL7 to CE. In order for Snomed to be able to apply post-coordination, the datatype will need to be changed to CD. An change request has been proposed to HL7 to enable this.

The type of specimen is identified. It may be EITHER:-

A Specimen conceptID taken from the

SNOMED lab specimen subset_UK_20060401

and optionally post-coordinated with

SNOMED lab topography subset_UK_20060401

SNOMED lab morphology subset_UK_20060401

SNOMED lab laterality subset_UK_20060401

according to the Principals laid down in " Principles for carrying Pathology Request and Result data as SnCT concepts"  (  NPFIT-FNT-TO-DPM-0378.01)

OR:-

An identified isolate taken from the SNOMED CT Concept  organism%
  Exc  domestic mammal -%
  Exc   Kingdom Animalia -%
  Exc   plant -%
  Exc   resistant organism -%
  Exc   trophic life form -%

(% = concept and all descendents; Exc = Excluding)

Resistant Organisms  (ESBL E.coli etc) will be carried as the Species with appropriate observations indicating the susceptibility pattern.

Isolates may be identified by Genus or to a Species level only. No post-coordination is allowed. All observations about the organism shall be carried as Observations off the SpecimenObservationCluster to which the Isolate is linked.

ActRef

References an act in the message using a unique identifier.

This is used where the identified specimen/Isolate is the product of another act carried within the message. This may occur if it is required to identify an act (e.g. Anaerobic Culture) that gave rise to an identified isolate. Not used in Requests.

This may only be used within the message and may not refer to any previously identified act that is not carried in the message.

[1..1] moodCode (CS {CNE:x_ActMoodIntentEvent} )

May be intent or Event. It is not used in Request mood in order to request that the Lab collect. Here it would be in Event mood as the decision that the lab shall collect has been made.

It may be in Intent mood where it has yet to be collected by the sender and they wish to indicate the time at which they intend to collect the specimen.

It is not intended that this is ever used to place an 'Order' for collection, merely to identify the collection process.
