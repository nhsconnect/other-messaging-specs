1    Overview

The Legitimate Relationship (LR) Messaging Service enables an electronic relationship to exist between a patient and either a Health Care Professional, NHS Organisation workgroup or another person. It ensures that security levels for access to patient records are maintained on LR Systems.

 

The message definitions accessible from within this document have been defined to support a number of business processes, namely:

  • Create a Legitimate Relationship
  • Change of Status of a Legitimate Relationship
  • Query Legitimate Relationship for specified patient with a Health Care Professional, workgroup, or Other Person (future use only)
  • Query Legitimate Relationships for specified patient.
  • Query Legitimate Relationship for a Health Care Professional, workgroup, or Other Person (future use only) with a list of patients
  • Query a user's User Role Profiles
  • Query a user's workgroup membership
  • Add and remove users from workgroups

1.1    Other Person

The inclusion of Other Person in messages throughout the Legitimate Relationships domain is for future use only. Other Person will not be processed by the NASP in this version of the MIM, and so must not be used.

 

 

2    Storyboards

2.1    Legitimate Relationship Creation, Confirmation, Details Query and Status Change

Mr. Chips falls from his ladder whilst painting his house on Christmas Day 2003.  He can hardly walk and is concerned that his leg might be broken.  He gets a taxi to the Accident & Emergency department at his local hospital.  The A&E receptionist who registers Mr. Chips asks him to sit down and wait.

 

The registration is recorded on the A&E registration system function which is part of an LSP-provided application.  The system function automatically creates a legitimate relationship for the combined A&E/radiology department Work Group, sending a Create Legitimate Relationship Request message for the self-referral. The relationship is confirmed by a Legitimate Relationship Creation Response, thereby enabling all the clinicians and clinical support workers in the Work Group to access Mr. Chips’ records.

 

The inclusion of radiology in this Work Group was a policy decision, made because X-rays are frequently needed for A&E patients, and A&E patients are invariably sent to radiology on an ad hoc basis not via a formal transfer.

 

Mr Chips’ name is called and he hobbles over to the desk where Sarah Fish, the A&E triage nurse, sits.  Sarah pulls up details about Mr. Chips on her computer screen: the registration details provided plus other clinical information about his previous NHS encounters and health (like the fact that he is a diabetic) that may be pertinent to the A&E visit.

 

Sarah enters identifiers for Mr. Chips in the system function designed for triage.  In order to verify the existence of the legitimate relationship with Mr. Chips, the A&E application automatically issues a Legitimate Relationship Confirmation Request to ensure that Sarah has a Legitimate Relationship with Mr. Chips. Since Sarah has logged on using a Role Profile containing the A&E/Radiography Work Group, the existence of this “active” relationship is confirmed by the NASP through a Legitimate Relationship Confirmation Short Response.  Following the confirmation, the application displays relevant clinical information about Mr. Chips, and allows Sarah to update his records.

 

The nurse asks him a few questions about his health and records these on the system.  She has a quick look at Mr. Chips’ leg, advises him that he should wait to see a doctor, but warns him that he has another three hours to wait. Sarah’s actions confirm that the self-referral has been accepted, and so the A&E application automatically issues a Legitimate Relationship Status Change Request to confirm the referral acceptance. A Legitimate Relationship Status Change Response message is returned to give confirmation that the state change was successful. Mr. Chips hobbles away and resumes his original seat.  Finally, Mr. Chips is asked to come through to speak to the A&E registrar, Julie Salt.  Julie has already called up Mr. Chips’ record.

 

In order to access the patient’s records, Julie Salt uses the same system function, with the same process of legitimate relationship confirmation, as was performed by Sarah Fish.

 

After a thorough examination, Dr. Salt records a few details and advises Mr. Chips that he should have an X-ray. He consents to this, and so Julie records an order request to radiology department.  A porter arrives with a trolley and Mr. Chips is moved and parked near the X-ray department to wait in another queue.

 

When used in other hospitals, the A&E clinical application might create a Legitimate Relationship between the patient and a Work Group whose identity is a managed policy value for that application. In this hospital there is no value set because there is no need to create the relationship since, by policy, the one created when the patient entered A&E already embraces the radiology department. In hospitals operating a policy in which Radiography and A&E are in different work groups the application might well be configured to create a new second Legitimate Relationship with a “Radiology” Work Group.

 

However in this hospital there is no Work Group specified so this particular order request does not require the creation of a new legitimate relationship to radiology.  

 

The radiographer confirms Mr. Chips’ identity and then pulls up on his screen the notes taken by Julie Salt.  He helps Mr. Chips into position, takes some X-rays, and asks a porter to wheel the patient back to A&E.

 

The radiographer enters the patient’s identifiers, and another Legitimate Relationship Confirmation Request is issued automatically by the radiology software.  A matching Legitimate Relationship Confirmation Short Response message is issued confirming the relationship with the radiographer, just as it had been for Sarah Fish and Julie Salt, enabling access to Mr. Chips' records. The radiology software integrates with PACS, allowing the X-rays to be stored digitally on the LSP system. 

 

The porter leaves Mr. Chips in a hidden corner of A&E.  He falls asleep for ten hours.  He wakes to realise he has been forgotten and shouts for attention.  Eventually he is seen by the A&E consultant, Dr. Mushpea, who looks up his details and reviews the X-rays of his leg.

 

The A&E software issues another Legitimate Relationship Confirmation Request for Dr. Mushpea. This legitimate relationship checking and creation is invisible to the user, Dr. Mushpea.

 

There is no fracture evident, so his leg is strapped. Dr. Mushpea discharges and says goodbye to Mr. Chips. The referral discharge is a significant legitimate relationship state-changing event, and so the application sends a Legitimate Relationship Status Change Request, which is confirmed through a Legitimate Relationship Status Change Response message being returned. The legitimate relationship remains active for a set period of time (controlled by the National Application Service Provider), providing the A&E team with further access if necessary.

 

In the days that follow, Mr. Chips decides he will complain about being neglected in A&E.  He decides to get hold of evidence from the hospital showing the time between being seen in radiology and being seen again by Dr. Mushpea.  He writes to the hospital with a Subject Access Request, asking for all information recorded over the previous year. A special section of the IT department in the local hospital (the “Subject Access Work Group”) processes all Subject Access Requests.  They receive the request and record it in their system.

 

The receipt of a Subject Access Request triggers the creation of a different type of legitimate relationship: a Subject Access Request Legitimate Relationship. A system function in the LSP software used by the IT Department to record Subject Access Requests automatically triggers a Create Legitimate Relationship Request.  A Legitimate Relationship Creation Response is returned, confirming the new relationship between Mr. Chips and the Subject Access Work Group.

 

Users who are members of the Subject Access Request Work Group process the request and output and collate all the information about Mr. Chips.

 

This includes extracting details of all the legitimate relationships between Mr. Chips and system users accountable to the hospital that were in a state of either “active” or “frozen” in the last year.  A system function within the LSP software allows for such a data enquiry (although it will not return any LRs which are now expired).  The user inputs a Legitimate Relationship Record Details Request and is returned a Complete Legitimate Relationship Record Details Response.

 

When the outputs have been posted to Mr. Chips, the IT Department records the closure of the Subject Access Request on the system.

 

The closure of the Subject Access Request changes the state of the legitimate relationship between the Subject Access Request Work Group and Mr. Chips. This is performed automatically when the closure is recorded by the LSP software which triggers a Legitimate Relationship Status Change Request. 

A Legitimate Relationship Status Change Response message is returned to give confirmation that the state change was successful.. The relationship remains active for a set period of time (controlled through national time-based parameter settings) before expiring automatically.

2.2    Batch Confirmation

At Good Health Hospital outpatient clinic (General Surgery), the clinic administrator, Susan Smith, needs to review the appointments in order to prepare for the clinic. In order to do this, she needs to have a legitimate relationship with the patients involved. All patients should already have a legitimate relationship of type 'Patient Referral' with the workgroup 'General Surgery - Outpatient clinic'. (They will also have an LR with the 'General Surgery' speciality Workgroup, from their original referral to the hospital.)  Susan calls up an appointment list of patients to be seen. In this case, there are 100 patients on the schedule, so rather than checking each patient individually, the application sends a Query Batch Confirmation Request to the NASP with 100 entries, one for each patient. Susan is a member of the 'General Surgery - Outpatient Clinic' workgroup, and as all patients on the clinic list have an LR with that workgroup, the NASP responds with a Confirm LR - Batch Response which contains positive confirmation for each patient specified in the request. The application then displays the full appointment list to Susan.

 

Meanwhile at the Plastic Surgery clinic, the consultant, Mr Daniels, needs to review the cases to be seen, in order to decide which he will see and which he will assign to his registrar. He selects the 'Case List' facility within the LSP application to see a summary list of the patients, which gives name, diagnosis and date of surgery, as a brief reminder of what has happened. When prompted to select which cases to view, Mr Daniels should choose the correct option for his clinic 'Plastic Surgery', but he inadvertently chooses 'All Clinics'. The application needs to check that he has a legitimate relationship with all the patients involved, so it sends a Query Batch Confirmation Request to the NASP. The NASP responds with a Confirm LR - Batch Response which indicates that only 15 out of 37 patients have LRs with the consultant. The application displays the whole list of 37 cases, however full details are only available for the 15 cases where there is a legitimate relationship; the details of the other 22 cases are blanked out. As there are missing records, Mr Daniels must take corrective action, according to defined procedures. For instance, the application could ask Mr Daniels if he would like to self-claim an LR with the missing patients, thereby triggering a set of alerts. However, in this case, he has experienced the problem before, so he simply goes back and selects the correct option, 'Plastic Surgery', triggering another Query Batch Confirmation Request. This time, the Confirm LR - Batch Response which is returned confirms that the consultant has an LR with all patients involved, and the application displays the case list that he expected.

2.3    Workgroup Membership Management

Kath Carroll arrives at Good Health Hospital to start work on her first day as a nurse on the Cardiology ward. In order to gain access to the records of the patients being cared for on the ward, which she will need in the course of her duties, the nurse must be added to the workgroup represented by the ward team. The ward administrator Christopher Clerk, who also acts as a Workgroup Membership Administrator (WGMA), opens the ward management application and initiates the function for adding a new staff member to the team. As part of this process, the application automatically initiates the process of adding Kath Carroll to the workgroup representing the ward team.

 

After checking that he has the appropriate access rights, Christopher is prompted to enter identifiers for the nurse into the system, and so he enters her name and SDS user ID. The LSP software then automatically issues an Identify User Role Profiles Query in order to determine Kath's current set of User Role Profiles. The NASP system responds with an Identify User Role Profiles Query Response which contains her current URPs. If there is more than one to choose from, the LSP software displays these URPs to Christopher so he can choose which one is appropriate, i.e. which one the nurse should be added to the workgroup under.

 

Once Christopher Clerk has chosen which URP to add the nurse to the workgroup under, he enters this choice into the LSP system. The LSP system then issues an Add User to Workgroup Request. When the NASP subsystem receives the request, it automatically performs a check to see if the user's WGMA powers extend to the specific workgroup chosen. It also checks whether the user is already a member of the workgroup before carrying out the request. The NASP subsystem responds with an Add User to Workgroup Response to confirm whether or not the nurse has been added to the workgroup. If the nurse was already a member the response would contain a warning to that effect. In this case, she is not already a member so the request is successfully carried out. Now, when Kath logs onto the system under the chosen URP, she can access the medical records of all patients under the care of the ward team, as she now has Legitimate Relationships with all these patients through her membership of the ward workgroup.

 

Some time later, Kath Carroll moves to another hospital, and so needs to be removed from the workgroup representing the ward team. The ward administrator opens the ward management application and initiates the function for removing a member of staff from the team. He chooses Kath's User Role Profile to remove. After checking that Christopher has the right to do this, the application automatically issues a Remove User from Workgroup Request to the NASP. After performing the same access control check as it did for joining, the NASP subsystem returns a Remove User from Workgroup Response to confirm that the nurse, acting in her URP, has been removed from the workgroup.

 

 

3    Application Roles

The applications involved in Legitimate Relationships processes play specific roles.  These, along with the interactions associated with each role, are identified below.

 

3.1    Legitimate Relationship Requestor - REPC_AR010000UK10

This application is capable of requesting the creation and status change of legitimate relationships, as well as requesting that users be added to or removed from workgroups.

 

Legitimate Relationships Requestor Interactions

 

3.2    Legitimate Relationship Receiver - REPC_AR020000UK10

The LR System performs two inseparable functions; it holds all the recorded LRs and acts as Query Fulfiller, providing details of LR records relating to specified patients in response to a retrieval request.

 

This application is capable of receiving requests from the Requestor application, acting on them (e.g. creating LRs, changing status, adding users to and removing users from workgroups), and sending the appropriate response or acknowledgement. In the role of Query Fulfiller, it is capable of providing details of LR records, User Role Profiles belonging to users and details of workgroup membership.

 

Legitimate Relationship System Interactions

 

Legitimate Relationship System as Query Filler Interactions

 

3.3    Legitimate Relationship Query Placer - QUPC_AR010000UK10

The Legitimate Relationship Query Placer is the initiator of a request to retrieve from the LR System, LR records for the patient identifier provided in a retrieval request.

 

This application is capable of sending any queries relating to LRs, and expects the Receiver to take the appropriate action. The query placer is responsible for generating query identifiers and matching them with responses.

7    Message Definitions

7.1    Create Request - REPC_MT010101UK15

This message allows an application, either automatically or on behalf of a user, to request a Legitimate Relationship be created between a specified patient and either an individual User Role Profile, workgroup or Other Person (future use only - should not be used in this version of the MIM).

 

The Other Person is only a placeholder for future use.

 

        

 

7.2    Confirm Request -REPC_MT020101UK11

This message is sent to the requestor by the LR System holding the Legitimate Relationships (LR) data, to confirm that a LR has been created.

 

           

 

7.3    Request Status Change - REPC_MT040101UK14

This message allows an application, either automatically or on behalf of a user, to request the status of Legitimate Relationship be changed between a specified patient and either an individual User Role Profile, workgroup or Other Person (future use only - should not be used in this version of the MIM).

 

           

 

7.4    Confirm Status Change - REPC_MT050101UK11

This message is sent to the requestor by the LR System holding the Legitimate Relationships (LR) data, to confirm that the status change has been successful.

 

           

 

7.5    Query LR Confirmation - QUPC_MT010101UK32

This message allows an application, either automatically or on behalf of a user, to query whether a LR exists for a specified patient.

 

The NHS number is a query parameter item. The query also sends the levels of response required, either ‘Short’ or ‘History’.

 

This message requires the UserID, UserRoleProfile and optional Workgroups or Other Person to be provided.

The Other Person is only a placeholder for future use.

   


           

 

7.6    Confirm LR - Short Response - REPC_MT070101UK13

This message is sent to the requestor by the LR System, to confirm whether a current (Active) LR exists for the patient whose NHS number was included in the search parameter. If an LR does exist then the value field shall be 'true'. If no LR exists then the value field shall be 'false'.

 

           

 

7.7    Confirm LR - History Response -REPC_MT080101UK30

This message is sent to the requestor by the LR System, to confirm whether a LR exists for the patient whose NHS number was included in the search parameter. The response shall indicate an existence of a LR record (Active, Inactive or Frozen) for the patient. If an Active LR does exist then the value field shall be 'true'. If no Active LR exists then the value field shall be 'false'.

 

           

 

7.8    Query LR Details -QUPC_MT020101UK15

The message notifies the LR System of a query message to retrieve and return the contents of all Legitimate Relationship records for the patient held on the database, in order to determine which User Role Profiles or workgroups are involved in a Legitimate Relationship with the specified patient for a (optional) specified time period.

 

Required parameters for this query are:-

  • patient NHS number
  • Response Format (Simple or Complete)
  • DissentOverride

 

The Time Period to which the LRs relate is an optional parameter. If no value entered, then all Legitimate Relationships which are Active, Inactive or Frozen will be returned in the response.

 

The LR's that shall be returned are Legitimate Relationships which are not expired and where:

LR Start Date & Time <= Request End Date and LR Expiry Date & Time is NULL or

LR Start Date & Time <= Request End Date and LR Expiry Date & Time => Request Start Date.

 

NB a user must have an existing Legitimate Relationship with a patient before being permitted to submit a Query LR Details for that patient.

 

           

 

7.9    LR Details - Simple Response - REPC_MT090101UK32

The message contains basic details for a Legitimate Relationship record held on the LR Database, showing the User Role Profiles or workgroups involved in Legitimate Relationships with the specified patient.

The Other Person is only a placeholder for future use.

8    Glossary of Terms

Legitimate Relationship A Legitimate Relationship is a relationship between a clinician (or NHS organisation work group) and a patient, that gives the clinician, or workgroup member, certain levels of entitlement to access that patient’s health record maintained on National Systems.
Health Care Professional A Clinician involved in the Legitimate Relationship.
Workgroup A team of clinicians who work together within an NHS organisation, to provide healthcare.
User Role Profile An individual user of the Spine may be able to operate in a number of different roles within a number of different NHS organisations. A User Role Profile (URP) exists for each of these roles within a given organisation, which consists of a set of attributes pertaining to that role. A URP may apply to only one user.
