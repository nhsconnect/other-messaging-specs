9.10.Remodel planned as

 

·         SBADM in INT mood when the patient has not yet been asked to take the medication

·         SBADM in RQO mood when the GP is requesting that the patient start taking the medication

·         There is not necessarily an INT statement, the initial statement may be an RQO statement for a one-off prescription or an EVN statement where a medication is administered without a prescription (e.g. in an emergency).

·         Both the INT and the RQO SBADM statements equate to the overall plan and their duration is the total duration of the plan rather than the duration dictated by the sufficiency of supply or supplies

·         Where there is an INT statement, the corresponding RQO statement updates the INT with an RPLC ActRef

§         This implements the plan, which can be seen to move from call-off to called-off, or from planned, to on-going

·         This therefore means that

§         a eTP prescription is a SPLY in RQO mood with a copy of the RQO SBADM in the care event report

·         There is one SBADM in RQO mood statement to many SPLY statements in RQO mood

·         Mood to be INT or RQO in PoC ( new x_domain required)
