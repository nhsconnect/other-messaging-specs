1      Purpose

The purpose of this document is to record some clinical storyboards to support the development of Message Examples for P1R2 Provision of Care (POC), namely the Care Event Report and Discharge Summary messages.

The storyboards (or scenarios) demonstrate the Primary & Secondary healthcare settings applicable to these POC messages.

3      Primary Care

3.1           Simple - Single Problem

3.1.1    Aims

This scenario aims to give an example of a simple (and common GP scenario). This consultation stands on its own, in that the problem described by the patient is new, it is dealt with in it entirety by the single visit.

 

3.1.2    Summary

Our patient, Paul Simon Smith, attends his GP, Dr Fixit, complaining of a sore throat. He is examined and prescribed antibiotics.

 

3.1.3    Detail

  • Paul has been suffering with a sore throat for 4 days. His symptoms are getting worse so he decides he needs to see his GP. He therefore telephones his local surgery and makes an appointment to see Dr Fixit the next day.
  • The following day Paul arrives at the surgery for his appointment. He books in with the surgery receptionist. And takes a seat in the waiting room.
  • Dr Fixit finishes documenting the consultation of his last patient. He looks at his surgery list; he sees that Paul is next to be seen. Dr Fixit looks at Paul’s medical history on his computer. He sees that Paul is fit and well and that the last time he attended the surgery was 2 years ago with an Upper Respiratory Tract Infection. He calls him in.
  • Paul enters the surgery and sits down.
  • Dr Fixit asks Paul what the Problem is.
  • Paul tells Dr Fixit that he has had a Sore throat for 5 days. He has pain on swallowing. Over the last 2 days he has become feverish. He does not have a cough. He has taken paracetamol and throat sweets.
  • Dr Fixit examines Paul. He examines Paul’s cervical lymph nodes and looks in his throat. He finds bilateral enlarged cervical lymph nodes, enlarged inflamed tonsils with exudates.
  • Dr Fixit diagnoses tonsillitis.
  • Dr Fixit asks Paul if he is allergic to any medication. He responds that he had a rash after taking penicillin about 10 years ago. Dr Fixit prescribes erythromycin tablets.
  • Dr Fixit advices Paul to take paracetamol 500mg 2 tabs qds for his fever and to drink plenty of fluids. He requests that Paul comes back to see him if he is no better in 3 days.
  • Paul leaves the surgery and heads for the local chemist to get his antibiotics and some more paracetamol.
  • Dr. Fixit completes his consultation notes. And moves on to the next patient.

3.2    Multiple Care Professionals

3.2.1    Aims

This scenario involves more than one Care Professional from the Primary Care Team. There are 3 Care Events which occur in this one visit to the surgery with 2 care professionals. Medication is administered.

 

3.2.2     Summary

James Smith has over the last 2 days been suffering with his asthma. His mum decides that he needs to see the doctor. Whilst he is at the doctors he is given a nebuliser and his medication dose is changed.

 

3.2.3    Detail

  • Shirley Smith notices that James is unwell. He has been coughing a lot over the last few days and this morning he is wheezy. She rings the surgery to make an appointment. She is given an appointment for later that day.
  • Shirley and James arrive at the surgery and book in with the receptionist.
  • Dr Justin Case a locum doctor is covering the surgery today. He looks at his surgery list and he sees that James is next to be seen. He has a look at James’s record and sees that James has Asthma which is usually well controlled on beclomethasone and salbutamol diskhalers. He calls James in.
  • Shirley and James enter the surgery and sit down.
  • Dr Case asks them what the problem is. Shirley tells the Doctor what has been happening over the last few days.
  • Dr Case examines James. He gets James to do a Peak flow test the result is low (best ever PEFR is 240 today it is 160)
  • Dr Case decides that James needs a nebuliser. He rings the practice nurse to request that she set this up.
  • James and Shirley leave Dr Case’s room and sit outside the Nurses Room. (end of Care Event 1)
  • Nurse Screens calls them into her room and administers the nebuliser. (Salbutamol 2.5mg)
  • Part way through the administration of the nebuliser James starts to feel unwell he complains he is felling dizzy. Nurse Screens stops the nebuliser which has nearly finished. She examines James and notices that he is flushed, she takes his pulse which is raised at 130 (regular rhythm). She is aware that this is a side effect of the nebuliser. She calms James and rechecks his pulse after 5 minutes it has reduced to 90.  James is now feeling much better. Nurse Screens asks James to repeat his peak flow again. This has come back to nearly his normal level (230). Nurse Screens phones Dr Case (who has meanwhile been seeing other patients) and asks him to review James.
  • James and Shirley sit back down in the waiting room (end of Care Event 2)
  • Dr Case finishes seeing his current patient. He calls James back in to see him.
  • James and Shirley enter the consultation room and sit down.
  • James tells Dr Case he feels much better. Dr Case examines his chest again and gets James to repeat his peak flow test (235). Dr Case is happy with this.
  • Dr Case consults his asthma guidance and sees that he needs to give James a 3 day course of steroids and to add a Salmeterol inhaler to his regular regime.
  • He advises Shirley that if James gets any worse again then he should call the surgery immediately. If he continues to get better then he should be review in 5 days (end of Care Event 3)

3.5     Home Visit

3.5.1    Aim

The representation of a home visit and recording of risks and warnings.

 

3.5.2    Summary

Dr Fixit is called to visit Frank Smith. Frank was registered with another practice in the local area but his family have decided they were not happy with the care he was receiving. Dr Fixit has agreed to take him as a patient. He goes to see him on a home visit.

 

3.5.3    Detail

  • Prior to going on the home visit Dr Fixit calls up Frank’s record. He has no record of his own as he is a new patient. There has been no GP2GP transfer as Frank’s old GP did not keep computerised records. The paper record has yet to arrive.
  • Dr Fixit arrives at Frank’s home he notices that the house is in a state of disrepair, he hears a dog barking.
  • Dr Fixit rings the door bell, the dog continues to bark and there is no answer.
  • Dr Fixit decides to try the door, he opens it slightly and the dog sticks his nose out. Dr Fixit is confident around dogs so he calms the dog and walks in.
  • Inside he finds Frank in a back room sitting in front of the TV which is turned up high.
  • He manages to attract Frank’s attention and the TV is turned off.
  • Dr Fixit spends time talking to Frank. Frank appears to be mildly confused. He is surprised that his dog has not gone for the doctor. He says that last week he bit the post man.
  • Dr Fixit asks if Frank is taking any medication or is seeing any other Health professional. Frank says that he was in the Local Mental Health hospital about 10 years ago.
  • Dr Fixit is worried about Frank and he feels that he needs to find out more.
  • Dr Fixit leaves Frank’s house and returns to his surgery, where he enters information about the Care Event

Care Record Element Information to be Recorded Notes
Problems and Issues Confused – unknown cause  
Risks to Care Professional or Third Party  Patient has a dangerous dog  
Risks to Patient Confused – may not be looking after himself  
Clinical Observations and Findings Confused – mini mental score

4.2     One Stop Clinic

4.2.1    Aim

Visit to an acute trust for a series of tests and consultations in one encounter.

 

4.2.2    Summary

Ethel Smith has been suffering from chest pain for a number of weeks. Her GP Dr Fixit decides to refer her to Dr Ticker’s Rapid Access Chest Pain clinic. She attends and is assessed.

 

4.2.3    Detail

  • Ethel has been suffering with Chest Pain for a number of weeks she has seen Dr Fixit (her GP) who has referred her to Dr Ticker’s Rapid Access Chest Pain Clinic (RACP).
  • Ethel arrives at the RACP clinic. She books in and takes a seat in the waiting room.
  • Nurse Clear is notified that Ethel has arrived. She calls Ethel into a consulting room.
  • Nurse Clear takes a history from Ethel and examines her. She confirms that Ethel conforms to the clinic’s referral criteria and that Ethel is fit enough to have an exercise ECG.
  • Nurse Clear takes blood from Ethel. (FBC, U&Es)
  • Ethel is then asked to go to the X-ray department for and Chest X-ray. She goes to the X-ray department and returns with he films (unreported).
  • Mrs Cardio Gram is informed that Ethel has returned from x-ray. She calls her into her consulting room. She performs a resting ECG. She confirms that he trace is essentially normal. Ethel then undergoes an exercise ECG. She then returns to the waiting area.
  • Dr Ticker is now available to see the next patient. He is informed that Ethel is next to be seen. He asks that Ethel be brought into his a joining consulting room. Meanwhile he reviews Ethel’s history and tests.
  • Dr Ticker goes into his consulting room. He confirms Ethel’s full history; he examines her cardiovascular and respiratory system.
  • From the history, examination and tests he confirms the diagnosis of angina. He arranges for Ethel to come in for angiography in 2 weeks. He prescribes aspirin, and atenolol. He arranges for Ethel to have fasting glucose and lipid tests the next day.
  • Nurse Clear gives Ethel some information about angina and the angiogram she is to have. And gives her smoking cessation advice.
  • Ethel leaves the hospital.

5    Inpatient

Note: Due to limited functionality of Acute systems, with specific respect to inpatient clinical noting, the scope of the inpatient discharge messages is therefore reduced. These messages will contain the equivalent information currently recorded in an immediate/interim discharge message. That is discharge medication, Major procedures performed, diagnoses made and follow up plan.

  • They agree she can go home. Dr Ian Training fills out Ethel Discharge note/message.
  • Ethel is taken home by Wilfred Smith (her husband).

5.3     Maternity

5.3.1    Aim

To show how discharge messages from a maternity setting should be handled in P1R2

 

5.3.2    Summary

Shirley Smith is admitted to the Central Delivery Unit of the Maternity Hospital in labour. After a short labour she delivers baby Mary. Mary and Shirley are both well and are discharged after 6 hours

 

5.3.3    Detail

  • Shirley Smith is pregnant this is her second pregnancy it has been uneventful so far her expected date of delivery is 18/3/2004.38 weeks pregnant.
  • Overnight Shirley has noticed that she has started to get some abdominal pains after a few hours she thinks these are contractions. She phones the Central Delivery Unit (CDU) of the Maternity Hospital. She speaks to Midwife Keep Pushing who advises her to come down to the CDU to be assessed.
  • Shirley arrives on the CDU
  • Midwife Keep Pushing confirms Shirley’s history and examines her. She confirms she is in labour and admits her.
  • Shirley’s normal labour continues and is relatively.
  • At 2230 4/3/2003 Shirley delivers a baby girl.
  • Mum and baby are examined and are found to be fit and well.
  • Baby is allocated an NHS Number
  • Mum and babe recover and are reassessed 6 hour post delivery
  • They are both still well and are discharged home
